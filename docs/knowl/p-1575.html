To find the matrices above more generally we note that we have \(\det(M) \equiv ad \equiv a(\trace M - a) = a\trace M - a^2 \pmod N\) and we are then solving \(a^2 - \trace M a + \det M \pmod N\) and finding \(b\) from this. Really we are looking at the generator of our order \(\ZZ\lb \alpha\rb\text{,}\) reducing modulo \(N\) and finding the \(a\) coefficient as this reduction, the all primes dividing \(N\) split condition then means that this all works out and we get a number we can put in our matrix.

Riemann-Hurwitz (generally)

There's nothing that doesn't generalise about the previous proof.

Claim 2.3.8

For \(\pi\colon R\to S\) a non-constant morphism of compact Riemann surfaces

\begin{equation*} \chi(R) = \deg \pi \cdot \chi(S) - \sum_{x\in S} (\deg(\pi) - \#\pi\inv(x))\text{.} \end{equation*}
Corollary 2.3.9

There are no non-constant morphisms from a sphere to a surface of genus \(\gt 0\text{.}\)

Let \(D\) be a fundamental discriminant of an imaginary quadratic field. If there exists a modular elliptic curve \(E\) (defined over \(\QQ\)) whose associated base change Hasse-Weil \(L\)-function

\begin{equation*} L_{E / \QQ(\sqrt{D})}(s) \end{equation*}

has a zero of order \(\geq 4\) at \(s=1\) then for every \(\epsilon>0\text{,}\) there exists an effective computable constant \(c_{\epsilon}(E)>0\text{,}\) depending only on \(\epsilon, E\) such that

\begin{equation*} h(D)>c_{\epsilon}(E)(\log |D|)^{1-\epsilon}\text{.} \end{equation*}

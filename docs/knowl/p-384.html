\(\cat{Isog}\) is a semisimple abelian category. The simples are simple abelian varieties.

  1. Decomposition up to isogeny into a product of simple abelian varieties is unique.
  2. If \(A\) is simple \(\End A \otimes \QQ\) is a division algebra over \(\QQ\text{.}\) Reason: If \(A\) is simple in an abelian category, if \(\End A \supseteq k\) a field implies it's a division algebra.

\(g \gt 1\) \(\mathcal M_g\) is a complex variety of dimension \(3g-3\text{.}\) Uniformization tells us that describing a Riemann surface amounts to specifying \(2g\) real \(2\times 2\) matrices \(\{\gamma_i\}_{i=1}^{2g}\) such that

  1. \(\det(\gamma_i) = 1\) which implies that \(\gamma_i\) depends on 3 real parameters so we have a total of \(6g\text{.}\)
  2. \(\prod_{i=1}^g \lb \gamma_i, \gamma_{g+i}\rb = \begin{pmatrix} 1\amp 0 \\ 0\amp 1\end{pmatrix}\) 3 relations, so \(6g- 3\text{.}\) Since for any \(\gamma \in \PSL_2(\RR)\) \(\Gamma = \langle \gamma_i \rangle\) and \(\gamma\Gamma \gamma\inv\) uniformize isomorphic Riemann surfaces implies \(6g-6\) real parameters, so \(3g-3\) complex parameters.
